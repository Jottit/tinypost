Our story

In 2005, Aaron Swartz and Simon Carstensen co-founded Infogami as part of the first Y Combinator batch. They wanted to make it easy for people to create and share their ideas on the web.

From that work came the original Tinypost in 2007, a simple way for anyone to make a website.

Nearly twenty years later, Tinypost is being rebuilt from scratch with the same idea at heart: a web that stays easy, open, and personal.

Tinypost lets anyone create a website instantly. There is no tracking, no followers, and no algorithms. Just your words on a small site that is yours.

It's open source, built on web standards, and designed to last. You can see how it works, fork it, or run your own version whenever you like.

If you'd like to support its development or help keep it independent, visit the sponsor page or email hello@tinypost.blog.
